 # Super-Store-Sales-Analysis
 
This project analyzes sales performance data from a Super Store dataset to uncover insights into sales, profit, customers, products, and regional performance. The analysis is supported by an interactive dashboard built in Excel to help stakeholders quickly understand business performance and make data-driven decisions.

# Objctive of Analysis

The main objectives of this analysis are to:
Identify the most preferred shipping method
Determine which regions generate the highest sales and profit
Evaluate product categories and sub-categories by sales and profit
Identify the most valuable customers
Highlight the top 10 selling and most profitable products
Analyze monthly sales and profit trends
Assess sales and profit by customer segment
Examine average sales performance by state

# Data Source

i. Source: Kaggle

ii. Dataset: Super Store Sales Dataset

iii. Data Type: Sales transaction data

iv. Time Period Covered: 2014 – 2017

# Tools Used

i. Microsoft Excel

ii. Data cleaning and preparation

iii. Pivot tables and pivot charts

iv. Slicers for interactivity (Region and Year)

v. Dashboard design and visualization

# Data Preparation & Cleaning

The following steps were carried out before analysis:

i. Removal of duplicate records

ii. Validation of missing or inconsistent values

iii. Formatting of date fields for time-series analysis

iv. Standardization of numerical fields (Sales, Profit, Quantity)

v. Creation of calculated fields such as Total Sales, Total Profit, Orders, and Profit Margin

# Dashboard Insights

### 6.1 Sales by Region

i. The West region recorded the highest sales performance.

ii. The South region generated the lowest sales among all regions.

iii. Regional slicers allow comparison across Central, East, South, and West.

### 6.2 Sales by Product Category

i. Technology is the highest-selling product category.

ii. Furniture and Office Supplies follow closely, showing balanced contributions to total sales.

### 6.3 Top 10 Customers by Sales

i. A small group of customers contributes significantly to total sales.

ii. Identifying these customers helps in targeting loyalty and retention strategies.

### 6.4 Monthly Sales Trend

i. Sales show fluctuations throughout the year.

ii. Peak sales are observed towards the end of the year, particularly in November and December.

iii. Early-year months record relatively lower sales volumes.

### 6.5 Sales by Sub-Category

i. Copiers and Phones are among the top-performing sub-categories by sales.

ii. Some sub-categories contribute lower sales but may still be profitable.

# Answers to Key Business Questions

i. Most Preferred Shipping Method: Standard Class (based on order frequency).

ii. Region with Highest Sales & Profit: West region.

iii. Product Category with Highest Sales & Profit: Technology.

iv. Most Valuable Customers: Customers with consistently high purchase values, as shown in the Top 10 Customers chart.

v. Top 10 Selling Products: Identified through sales by sub-category and product-level analysis.

vi. Most Profitable Products: Products with high profit contribution, particularly within the Technology category.

vii. Monthly Sales & Profit Trend: Increasing trend towards year-end.

viii. Total Sales by Customer Segment by Profit: Consumer segment contributes the most to overall profit.

ix. Average Sales by State: States such as California and New York record higher average sales.

# Conclusion
The Super Store Sales Analysis reveals strong performance driven by the West region, Technology products, and a small group of high-value customers. Seasonal trends indicate higher sales at year-end, highlighting opportunities for targeted promotions.

# Recommendations

i. Focus marketing and inventory efforts on high-performing regions and categories.

ii. Strengthen relationships with top customers through loyalty programs.

iii. Optimize shipping methods to improve customer satisfaction and reduce costs.

iv. Leverage seasonal trends to maximize revenue during peak months.


# Limitations & Future Work

i. The analysis is limited to historical data (2014–2017).

ii. Future analysis could include forecasting, customer lifetime value, and deeper profitability analysis by state and product
